3. Glossary Example

Biology / Computer Science
Biology is the study of living organisms, including their structure, function, growth, and evolution.
Computer Science is the study of computers, algorithms, programming, and how technology solves real-world problems.
Chemistry
Chemistry is the branch of science concerned with substances, their properties, and how they combine or react with one another.
It helps us understand the composition of matter and chemical reactions in daily life.
Physics
Physics is the study of matter, energy, and the fundamental forces of nature.
It explores concepts such as motion, energy, light, sound, and the laws governing the universe.
